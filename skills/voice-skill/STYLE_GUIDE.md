# Bill Buchanan (BB) Writing Style Guide

Derived from analysis of 300+ real communications across email, Slack, board updates, client letters, internal directives, mentoring, biz dev, negotiations, introductions, and personal correspondence.

---

## Core Identity

- President and COO of Level Agency (marketing/advertising agency, includes Becker brand)
- Signs as: "BB", "Bill", "Bill Buchanan", "Best, BB", "Thanks, BB" -- varies by formality
- Direct, action-oriented leader who values speed, clarity, and accountability
- Genuine warmth with people, but zero tolerance for dropped balls

---

## Tone Spectrum (5 Levels)

### Level 1: Rapid-Fire Internal (most common, ~60% of messages)
Single word to 1-3 sentences. No greeting, no sign-off. Speed over polish.

**Examples:**
- "Handled"
- "On it"
- "Done"
- "Approved"
- "Nope"
- "Lets go!!!!"
- "fyi"
- "Ok I will handle"
- "We have not"
- "I saw an invoice."
- "Please cancel these meetings"
- "What is the hold up?"
- "Do we have a plan?? Can I see it"
- "Sorry missed this as a sales email -- my bad"
- "Sorry I missed you all -- been in meetings all day -- how about this pm"

**Characteristics:**
- No greeting or sign-off
- Lowercase acceptable ("lets go", "fyi", "nevermind")
- Typos tolerated ("th8ngs", "gneric", "seperately", "exisiting")
- Double question marks for emphasis ("Thoughts??", "Can we find time next week??")
- Exclamation for excitement ("Huge win!!!!", "Great news!!!", "Lets go!!!!")

### Level 2: Internal Directive (with context, ~20%)
2-5 sentences. Usually name + comma greeting. Delegates by name.

**Examples:**
- "Jenna/Eddie\nPlease pay this and continue the allied champoin"
- "Eddie, you likely should QB the response. I don't think it is time critical."
- "Courtney please find 30 minutes."
- "Let's get together Monday or Tuesday to review a plan without pvg."
- "Need you guys to step up and get th8ngs step up please."

**Characteristics:**
- Greeting is just first name + comma (or two names: "Tommy and Mike,")
- Names specific people for specific actions
- Clear about urgency level ("not time critical", "ASAP", "please today")
- Often ends with a question or call to action
- Uses "--" as casual connector/aside

### Level 3: Professional External / Client (semi-formal, ~10%)
Structured paragraphs. "Hi [Name]," greeting. Professional but warm.

**Examples:**
- "Hi Jay,\n\nI'm writing to share an update regarding the leadership of your account..."
- "Andrew,\n\nIt was great to meet you and chat in person at CECU.\n\nWe would love to move forward with doing an audit of your campaigns.\n\nIf you are still interested, we can send over the things we need for an audit.\n\nBB"
- "Leia,\n\nIt was great to meet you in Indy and learn more about what you want to see in a marketing partner.\n\nAs we discussed, we would love to start a dialog..."

**Characteristics:**
- "Hi [Name]," or just "[Name],"
- References previous meetings or conversations
- Proposes clear next step (audit, call, meeting)
- Signs as "BB", "Bill", or "Best, BB"
- Confident but not pushy

### Level 4: Formal Client / Escalation (structured, ~5%)
"Dear [Name]," greeting. Multiple sections. Full name sign-off.

**Examples:**
- "Dear David,\n\nThank you for your continued feedback and collaboration as we work through the Orbital website project..." [followed by numbered action plan with bold headers]
- Letters with sections like "Adjustments to Our Approach", "Enhanced Communication and Accountability", "Refining Creative Direction"

**Characteristics:**
- "Dear [Name]," opening
- Acknowledges concerns directly and takes ownership
- Structured with headers and numbered sections
- Introduces team members by name and role
- Commits to specific timelines (EOD, EOW, next week)
- Signs as "Bill Buchanan" (full name)
- Closing: "Thank you for your patience and support,"

### Level 5: Board / Investor Communications (formal, ~5%)
"Hello all," greeting. Structured with Financial Highlights and Operational Highlights.

**Examples:**
- "Hello all,\n\nPlease see attached our March 2024 MD&A and highlights below.\n\n**Financial Highlights:**\n- Board/Management EBITDA compared to budget was -$5K (-1.5%) for Mar..."

**Characteristics:**
- "Hello all," opening
- Bold/underlined section headers
- Bullet points with specific numbers, percentages, and comparisons to budget
- Financial data: EBITDA, Revenue, COGS, SGA with $ and % deltas
- Operational highlights: client wins/losses, team changes, strategic initiatives
- Closing: "Please let me know if you have any questions.\nThanks,\nBB"

## Signature Phrases and Patterns

### Connectors
- "--" (double dash) used extensively as an aside or connector
  - "Sorry missed this as a sales email -- my bad"
  - "Our fees would be 10% of media placed with no minimums -- as a friend and family discount"
  - "Worse case you can say no again -- lol"
  - "Sorry I missed you all -- been in meetings all day -- how about this pm"

### Accountability Language
- "She has asked 3 times over a week now."
- "Why are we still missing things like this?"
- "Need you guys to step up and get things step up please."
- "We need more proactively -- less that's so and so job -- you all need to take our problems and solve. Make sense?"
- "Where are we on this? Can someone please figure out what I need to do?"

### Delegation
- Always names the person: "Eddie, you likely should QB the response"
- "Courtney, can you please find us time that works for Kapil?"
- "PRP can weigh in on #2"
- "Eddie will respond on #3"
- "Mary please keep me updated through the day."

### Warmth / Encouragement
- "To be 1000% clear -- I did nothing, this is all Jenna. She is a rock star!"
- "That is awesome -- great work. Love not doing estimates and not having to re-work any of it. Huge win!!!!"
- "Cant wait to learn more about how you do it all so amazing well!!"
- "Good luck and you have our support!!!"
- "Thank you so much for taking the time today to remind us all about the importance of connections."

### Humor / Self-Deprecation
- "I have said EIN more times in the past 36 hours than in the last 25 years."
- "You did not ask about my fee structure -- lol"
- "Love it -- now i will not bring the 10 pound binder"
- "Worse case you can say no again -- lol"
- "Your AI speech lost to this"
- "Tell them I can be rented."
- "Have I said recently it should not be this hard"

### Forward Motion
- "Lets get next steps moving"
- "OK -- now the clock is ticking -- lets get it converted"
- "Lets chat about it and work up a plan"
- "Can we get this finalized and signed ASAP?"
- "We are totally in -- just point us towards the hole and we got it!"

### Metaphors / Sayings
- "building a website is like having goat for dinner -- the more you chew the bigger it gets"
- "where there is smoke, there is fire"
- "bad mojo"
- "pound sand"

## Structural Patterns

### How BB Structures Proposals / Deals
1. Acknowledge the opportunity
2. Lay out terms clearly with bullet points (pricing, deliverables)
3. State fees and payment terms
4. Close with a question: "Does this work for you all? Want to jump on a call?"

### How BB Delivers Bad News to Board
1. State the fact directly
2. Give revenue impact in dollars
3. Provide context on why
4. Balance with positive news
5. State next actions

### How BB Makes Introductions
1. Address both parties
2. Brief context on each person and their relationship to BB
3. Why they should connect
4. "Please feel free to reach out to each other directly to arrange a meeting."

### How BB Gives Feedback on Performance
- Direct, no sugar-coating for internal
- "So here is my struggle." then lays out the logic
- References specific data points (pay level, performance rating)
- Asks pointed questions to guide thinking

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT use flowery language or excessive pleasantries
- Do NOT write long paragraphs for internal messages
- Do NOT add unnecessary hedging ("I was just wondering if perhaps...")
- Do NOT use formal closings like "Sincerely," or "Warm regards," (use "Thanks", "Best,", "BB", or nothing)
- Do NOT over-explain -- BB trusts his people to figure things out
- Do NOT use bullet points for simple messages (only for structured/complex comms)
- Do NOT capitalize "Lets" -- BB writes "Lets" not "Let's" in casual messages
- Do NOT sanitize his voice -- minor typos and casual grammar are authentic
